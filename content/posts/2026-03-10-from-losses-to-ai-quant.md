In 2019, I bought crypto for the first time.

Bitcoin was around $5,000–$6,000. I put in some money and went straight to 100x leverage. No strategy, no stop-loss — just one thought: *this thing is going up.*

A few days later, it was gone.

I told myself: tuition fee for a beginner. Normal.

---

## The Two Nights That Made Me Think I'd Figured It Out

Late 2020. I came back.

Bitcoin started moving up from the low $20,000s. I went heavy. Over two or three nights, my account went from $2,000 to over $100,000 — riding the move from $22k to $30k+, amplified by high leverage.

Those nights, I sat watching the numbers jump on my screen, convinced I finally understood this market.

Then over the next few days, almost all of it was gone.

---

## The Five Years After That

From 2021 onwards, I fell into a cycle that's hard to describe.

Up a little — can't hold, get out. Drops back — buy the dip, get stuck. Break even — add size, drops again.

Not one clean wipeout. Just grinding, slowly, year after year.

I studied technical analysis. Learned indicators. Tried strategy after strategy. But every time I sat in front of the screen, none of it mattered.

Up meant greed. Down meant fear. When I needed to cut losses, I couldn't. When I should have walked away, I chased.

I remember countless late nights staring at charts, telling myself: *this time I'll stay disciplined.* Then on the very next candle, doing the exact opposite.

Eventually I understood something clearly: **the problem was never the strategy. It was me.**

Greed and fear aren't flaws — they're instincts. No amount of willpower can sustainably override your own instincts.

---

## The Turning Point

Retail traders don't lose to institutions because of worse information or less effort.

They lose because institutions run systems, and retail runs on emotions. While I was agonizing at midnight over whether to cut a position, an algorithm on the other side had already executed.

In early 2026, I discovered AI-powered quantitative trading — and made a decision:

**Hand over every part of trading that requires overcoming human nature. Give it to AI.**

Let the model read the data. Let the algorithm decide direction. Let the program execute and manage risk. My only job: make sure the logic of the system is sound.

This wasn't just switching trading methods. It was the first time I felt like an individual could genuinely compete with institutions — system against system, on equal terms.

---

## Starting From Zero — 50 Days

When I made that decision, I couldn't write a single line of code.

January 20, 2026. I started. Ten hours a day, every day. Starting with basic AI chatbots, then moving to more capable models, then finding Claude — the AI tools themselves kept evolving, helping me build something I couldn't have touched alone.

I tried multiple model architectures: Random Forest, LightGBM, Transformer, TFT, ensemble approaches... each chosen for a reason, each eventually abandoned for a reason. The final system uses a lightweight Transformer, trained end-to-end from raw data to live execution.

In under two months, it went live.

At the same time, I deployed OpenClaw — an AI agent running 24/7 on my server, handling research, workflows, and helping run this blog. The quant system handles trading. OpenClaw handles everything else. Two AI systems running in parallel — that's how I work now.
