*Five years of crypto losses. One decision to change everything.*

---

2019. Bitcoin at $5,000–$6,000.

I put money in. Went straight to 100x leverage.

Days later, gone.

---

2020. I came back.

In two nights, my account went from $2,000 to over $100,000.

Bitcoin moving from $22k to $30k. High leverage. Perfect timing.

I thought I finally understood the market.

Then, over the next few days — almost all of it, gone.

---

What followed was five years of something worse than losing everything at once.

Up a little. Down a little. Up a little. Down a little.

Year after year.

I studied charts. Learned every indicator. Changed strategies constantly.

But every time I sat in front of the screen, none of it mattered.

Greed when it went up. Fear when it went down. Couldn't cut losses when I should. Chased entries when I shouldn't.

Countless nights I told myself: *this time I'll stay disciplined.*

Then on the very next candle, I did the opposite.

---

Eventually I understood something clearly.

**The problem was never the strategy. It was humanity.**

Greed and fear aren't flaws. They're instincts.

No willpower sustains itself against its own instincts. Not for long.

---

Retail doesn't lose to institutions because of worse information.

It loses because institutions run systems. Retail runs on emotion.

While I was agonizing at midnight over whether to cut a position, an algorithm on the other side had already executed.

In early 2026, I saw this clearly for the first time.

And made one decision: **hand over everything that requires overcoming humanity. Give it to the algorithm.**

Let the system read the data. Let the system decide. Let the system execute.

My only job — make sure the logic is sound.

For the first time, I felt like an individual could genuinely compete. System against system. Same starting line.

---

When I made that decision, I couldn't write a single line of code.

January 20, 2026. I started anyway.

Ten hours a day. From basic indicators to machine learning to deep learning — models changed, systems rebuilt from scratch, again and again.

In under two months, it went live.

---

This blog is the record of everything from here.

Every trade. Every mistake. Every moment the system gets it wrong — and right.

No guarantees. Just honesty.
